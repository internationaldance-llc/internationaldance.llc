    ABOUT

    International Dance, LLC. was founded as the School of International Dance by Christina Heimlich in 1962. The school is directed by Elizabeth Hawkins. Assistant director Rebecca Collier and our instructors are Miss Elizabeth, Miss Becca, Miss Sarah, Miss Victoria, Miss Natalie, and Miss Misha.

    Within the school we have a group of advanced dancers called "The Christina Heimlich Dancers." They perform and carry on the traditions that uphold the standards of our founder.

    We want to take this opportunity to let you know a little more about our program. As a student, your child will learn about pre-ballet or ballet, creative movement as well as international folk and character dance. Some classes also study tap and jazz. Participation in our classes will encourage development of motor coordination skills, balance, partnering, flexibility, strength, concentration, independence, creativity through use of imagination, poise, posture, rhythm, discipline - all while having fun and learning about music and dance.

    We have a deep, rich, and inspirational 60 year history and tradition of teaching dance to thousands of students, many for an average of 12 years. There is an expectation of excellence and professionalism associated with International Dance, LLC and the Christina Heimlich Dancers. With hundreds of original choreographed dances and well over one thousand handmade dance costumes, our talented dance teachers and dedicated parents are a rare treasure unique to International Dance, LLC.

    Mission Statement

    Our mission at International Dance is to provide quality, tasteful dance instruction in a positive environment. Our goal is to cultivate young dancers into well-rounded individuals, who have not only an appreciation for dance, but who reach for high achievement in academics and community service as well. Our instruction is diverse, age-appropriate, genuine, and fun.

    We want to make sure each student is well versed in various genres of dance. Equally important to dance technique, we strive to instill life skills like respect, teamwork, and the importance of community service. We aim to inspire and shape our students to be better artists and people both inside and outside of the studio. We at International Dance recognize anyone at any age can learn to love dancing. We are excited to stretch and enhance the ability of each individual dancer so that they can reach their personal goals.

    POLICY

    Overview

    Classes are held in Alexandria, Virginia and are available for ages three years to adult. Classes run from 30 minutes to 2 hours in length. Participation in our classes develops coordination and motor skills, balance, flexibility, strength, concentration, poise, posture, and discipline while having fun and learning about music and dance.

    International Dance's designed method and quality program follows a structured sequence of training stages intended to increase your dancer's technical skills, stamina and discipline in accordance with their age and physical development. We work hard to emphasize correct placement in order to prevent injury. Our curriculum embraces traditional ballet principles and incorporates elements of the French, Italian, and Russian training systems. These methods help to provide a healthy and enjoyable learning environment and allow the dancers to experience a feeling of mastery in each level. It is a style that readily adapts to meet the demands of any choreographer, any company, and any style of movement. Our strength is in our personal and individual attention with each student, training them to strive for excellence.

    Method of Assessment (class placement)

    All new and returning students will be evaluated for placement. Each dancer will be carefully placed at a level that ensures maximum progress. Although students are placed according to age for beginning levels, students with previous training may be placed into classes depending on their ability, strength, and previous development. Final placement is at the discretion of the Director and instructors.

    Advancement depends upon application, attitude, artistic abilities, dance accomplishment, and physical qualifications, and not necessarily upon age, or length of time spent studying dance. Students will be assessed on ability and accomplishment of certain expectations before being invited to participate in a higher level of training. We keep our program consistent with worldwide expectations and age-appropriate development.

    Intermediate Ballet: I and II

    In Intermediate I Ballet, attention is paid to the proper extension of the legs and shape of the feet, developing movement sequences, port de bras and balance. Dancers will see an increase in difficulty in barre and center work. Dancers begin to focus on repetition and retention. Intermediate Ballet training becomes much more serious. Some classes will focus on pointe work, pirouettes, use of the head and upper body, fluidity of port de bras and the intricacies of small and large jumps. International Folk and Character technique and combinations are developed with focus placed on personal presentation.

    Intermediate II Ballet continues the dancers' education. Level II keeps the attention and maintains the level of everything in Intermediate Ballet I while adding more repetitions, longer exercises, increased pointe strengthening, and challenging each dancer to increase their knowledge of dance vocabulary as well as a combination of exposure to classic ballets, ballet variations and styles. Each Intermediate student will be given the opportunity to push themselves. Within Intermediate Ballet II we see dancers start to become well rounded performers.

    Advanced Ballet: I and II

    Advanced I Ballet becomes more rigorous. Classes focus on pointe work, multiple pirouettes, fluidity of movement and the intricacies of small and large jumps. This class focuses on fine details, use of the upper body, breath of movement and expression, as well as an increasingly advanced repertory of steps. Focused attention is given to each advanced dancer enhancing their strengths and broadening their abilities. Character Dance is integral to a classical ballet repertoire. It is a stylized representation of traditional folk or national dance, normally from European countries and uses movements and dance which have been adapted for the theatre. Many classic ballets include and feature character dance. It is an integral part of International Dance, LLC.

    Advanced II Ballet focuses on all aspects introduced in Advanced Ballet I and more. Students are expected to be the utmost professional during class and these classes will prepare them for post high school. Advanced Ballet II will also focus on more classical variations. Dancers are expected to pick up choreography more quickly and delve into their artistry.

    Lyrical: I, II, and III

    Lyrical dance combines elements of ballet, jazz, and modern with a huge emphasis on emotions. Lyrical is typically performed to songs with lyrics and is danced with movement to convey the emotion of the song. Dancers in lyrical will enjoy movements that focus on flexibility and fluidity.

    Lyrical I
    Lyrical I is an introductory class to lyrical. Dancers will begin exploring emoting through movement while continuing to build on their ballet technique. Dancers will begin training their bodies to move in more parallel, contracted, and flexed positions. Dancers should be able to correctly execute basic lyrical movements such as single pirouettes from a parallel position, basic seat rolls and shoulder rolls, basic leaps and jumps, and be able to show the appropriate emotion through the movements taught.

    Lyrical II
    Dancers will continue to build upon basic lyrical moments while adding more complex and intricate movement. Dancers will continue to improve upon previously learned leaps, jumps, rolls, and turns as well as adding more difficult variations. Dancers should be able to correctly execute moments such as double pirouettes from a parallel position, a wider variety of leaps and jumps, more complex variations of seat and shoulder rolls, understand the basics a la seconde, and be able to show a wider range of emotions through the moments taught.

    Lyrical III
    Dancers will be challenged to explore more advanced lyrical movements. We will introduce improve and student choreography, as dancers will be asked to choreograph their own few 8 counts to end a given combination. Dancers should be able to correctly execute movements such as a polished double and triple pirouette from a parallel position, more complex leaps and jump combinations, more complex variations of seat and shoulder rolls, an a la seconde turning combination, and be able to show a wide range of emotions through the movement taught and be able to appropriately emote through those movements.

    Jazz/Modern Rotating Class

    Intermediate Jazz/Modern
    Students will learn the fundamentals of jazz dance technique while also exploring different styles of jazz through combinations each week. Students will learn the basics of modern dance as well as the history and the verbiage used in class.

    Advanced Jazz/Modern
    Students will expand their jazz terminology while reviewing the fundamentals of jazz dance technique, while also exploring different styles of jazz through combinations each week. Students will enhance and strengthen their creativity while reaching beyond the basics of modern dance as well as the history and the verbiage used in class. Students will be exposed to modern dance pioneers and their techniques.

    Tap Beginner Combo Classes, Intermediate, Advanced

    Beginner Combo Tap
    Class allows dancers to experience the sound of dance. Making rhythm with their feet brings smiles to their faces. Steps and terminology are introduced. Dancers will explore coordination, single time steps, beginning turns, short combinations, and showmanship along with rhythm and timing within a wide range of musical accompaniment.

    Intermediate Tap
    Class continues the development of technique & terminology. Dancers will further develop skills, including musicality and sound clarity, with an added focus on increasing speed and developing style. Dancers are introduced to tap legends and are exposed to musical theatre dancing and hoofing technique. Intermediate level students should expect to learn double and triple time steps, 3 & 5 beat riffs, pull-backs, pick-ups, more advanced turns, as well as more intricate rhythms.

    Advanced Tap
    Advance Tap level tappers should expect to master double and triple time steps, traveling time steps, 4 & 6 beat riffs, double pull-backs, pick-ups, more advanced turns, as well as more intricate rhythms and improvisation. Choreography and solo work are encouraged.

    Adult Ballet and Tap

    Adult Ballet
    Adult Ballet is all about getting adults moving and stretching. We focus on spotting which helps with getting less dizzy as well as strengthening the joints and ligaments. The rewards of taking ballet and tap as an adult will help you for years to come; including poise, core strength, and a decreased susceptibility to falling that may cause physical harm. The teacher will assess what kind of level each adult is and will create a plan on how to cater to each student.

    Adult Tap
    Adult Tap is a time where we use our feet to create fun and interesting rhythms. We like all types of music, (including 80's)! This class is meant to be pure fun and to get your heart rate up. We'll focus on across the floor tap technique and learn a dance combo to our favorite jams. This class will bring a smile to your face and will make you want to come back for more! The teacher will assess what kind of level each adult is and will create a plan on how to cater to each student.

    Behavior

    International Dance strives to provide a safe and supportive community that fosters a lifelong love for dance and creative expression. As such, International Dance expects each and every dancer, assistant, teacher, instructor, staff member and or parent/guardian to respect and be kind to each other at all times. International Dance does not tolerate bullying in any form; physical, emotional, social or otherwise.

    International Dance defines bullying as any behavior; physical, verbal, written (electronically or in person), that purposely and detrimentally targets another dancer, assistant, teacher, instructor, staff member and or parent/guardian.

    Reports of bullying will be investigated, verified, and documented by International Dance staff members. This investigation may include but is not limited to contacting the alleged bullying party, the alleged victim, teachers, assistants, and parent/guardians who may have possibly witnessed the event(s). If staff investigation produces corroborating evidence of the report, it will be documented and serve as the basis for a course of action. International Dance director, Elizabeth Hawkins, also reviews any and all reports of bullying.

    Verified and documented reports may have severe consequences up to and including immediate termination of enrollment without refund for classes missed.

    As per the policy above, International Dance recognizes that bullying does not just occur between dancers. Parents or guardians who are witnessed as bullying or harassing another dancer, assistant, teacher, instructor, staff member and or parent/guardian, are equally subject to the above policies and consequences.

    Here at International Dance we know how we behave specifically affects the learning environment. With a respectful, kind, safe atmosphere, anything is possible. Let's choose to be kind.

    Opening Classroom Doors

    Please respect our instructors. All classes are closed sessions. If the door is closed, do not open the door during class time without staff approval. When classes are over, the instructor will open the door for the next class to enter and dancers will exit to the lobby.

    Inclement Weather

    In the event of inclement weather, International Dance will follow Fairfax County Public Schools for closings. In the event of a delayed opening, the school will (in most cases) open on time. The final decision regarding closures resides with the Director of International Dance. A message will be placed on the website home page and an email will be sent out to registered dancers/parents when the studio is closed. Parents/dancers are responsible for ensuring the school has their current email address. Please refer back to 6.7 makeup classes.

    Illness

    Parents are advised to keep their dancer at home if they are experiencing any unexplained or undiagnosed pain. If they have a runny nose (green mucus indicates infection), watery eyes, coughing, sore throat, productive cough, fever (100°F/38.3°C or more), nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, or difficulty breathing (wheezing or a persistent cough), please keep your dancer home. Please refer back to 6.7 makeup classes.

    Lost and Found

    The lost and found is located in the round fabric bin in the Girls Dressing Room at the studio. Please check the office for lost jewelry or hair items. Items will be donated at winter, spring, and summer breaks and when the bin becomes overfull. Please check these bins regularly. International Dance is not responsible for lost or stolen items.

    Photography Release

    International Dance uses photographs and videos of its own dancers in its marketing and promotional materials. However, we will not include names of any dancers under the age of 18 beside a photograph or video unless specific permission from a parent/guardian has been obtained. By enrolling your child you are granting International Dance full permission to take, use, reproduce, and publish photographic, video, and digital images of your child for International Dance marketing and promotional purposes during and after the dancer's participation in our various programs. If you do not wish to have photographs or videos of your dancer(s) used in marketing and promotional materials or otherwise want this photography and video release to not apply to your dancer(s), you must opt out in writing.

    Withdrawing

    Our goal is to honor our families' varying priorities. If you are thinking about withdrawing for any reason, we request that you speak with us first. We would like to hear your concerns and want an opportunity to make the situation right. The solution may be as simple as changing classes. If a solution is not agreed upon, withdrawal is allowed. We require the current month and next month's tuition to be paid before withdrawal is complete.

    Recital Performances

    Performances are held each spring for our annual recital. In addition to the Spring Recital, we carry on the tradition of our International Christmas Performance, which has delighted audiences for years. We have also performed the half-time show for the Harlem Globetrotters, International Day events and in the past at the Green Spring Nursing Home throughout the year, spreading "happiness through dancing."

    All families are asked to volunteer in some way to support the dance season's performances. Giving back and learning more about the rich history of International Dance is part of experiencing each unique event.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long are the recital performances?

    Each performance is approximately 1.5-2.5 hrs.

    Will my dancer get to sit in the audience once their dance is finished?

    For general safety reasons, we insist that all performers remain backstage during the recital performances. Depending on the venue during rehearsals students of International Dance may be allowed to sit with their class and watch the other classes. We have plenty of staff backstage to keep your dancers entertained while they wait for their dance. If your dancer is in our youngest classes, we may have the dancers sit with parents after their performance. We will communicate with parents so that this can be a smooth transition.

    What if my dancer is in more than one dance?

    We have great backstage help that will assist your dancer with her costume changes while you relax and enjoy the show.

    Does my dancer have to wear makeup to dress rehearsal?

    Your dancer does not have to wear makeup to dress rehearsal, however, we do recommend some makeup for the recital, so the stage lighting does not make dancers appear "washed out."

    Will there be someone taking pictures of my dancer during the rehearsal and recital?

    We do hire a professional photographer to take pictures during the show; you are welcome to take pictures of your dancers from your seat only; however, flash photography is not permitted during the performances.

    Will there be a DVD to purchase?

    We require all families to pay a media technology fee paid at time of registration. It includes digital downloads of the performances as well as a sitting fee for picture week. Additional digital downloads of the performances or past performances are available per request. Details are sent out in performance information emails.
